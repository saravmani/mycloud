separation of concerns (SoC) is a design principle for 
separating a computer program into distinct sections,
such that each section addresses a separate concern.


 is the process of breaking a computer program into distinct features that overlap in functionality as little as possible

 Seperation of Concerns states that applications should be split in to modules whose functionalities overlap as little as possible.


  is the process of breaking a computer program into distinct features that overlap in functionality as little as possible. A concern is any piece of interest or focus in a program. Typically, concerns are synonymous with features or behaviors.


  uses
  ----------
  When concerns are well-separated, individual sections can be reused, as well as developed and updated independently
